Ghanpokhara is a fascinating Gurung village, blessed with unrivaled spectacular splendors and home to the Ghales and Gurungs, who are the renowned Gurungs of western Nepal's mid-hill people. A unique ethnic culture's repository Ghanpokhara is a microcosm of breathtaking natural beauty combined with a traditional ethnic way of life. Ghanpokhara is most popular tour which is located east of the Marshyngdi River, between Gorkha and Lamjung district. The trail starts from Khudi via Besisahar to Ghalegaon and Ghanpokhara. The Villages are predominantly habited by Gurungs of this region.The trail passes through other Gurung villages, Bhujung, Pasgaun, Pakhrikot, Nangidhar finally ending at Pokhara. Visitors can see spectacular panoramic views of Annapurna range,Dhaulagiri,Manaslu,Lamjung and Hiunchuli, if weather permits.Hospitality of the people out there is overwhelming. Bird watching at Talangyo Lake, Utter Kanya Temple, Pempro Fall, Batase Cave, Khudi hydroelectric dam site, and the tea garden at Ghalegaun are some of the most popular attractions.The local communities in these villages perform cultural shows for visitors on request. Accommodation is available at community-run home-stays.The local people also perform traditional Ghatu,Ghaybru,Krishnacharitra,Jhankri/lama and many local cultural dances for visitors dressed in traditional attire.

Access and accommodation: The most common route to reach Ghale Gaun is through Besisahar-Baglungpani-Kapur Gaun route and is a 7-hour drive from Kathmandu. You can find community homestays and teahouses all around the village.There are three different ways to reach the village, each trail passes through the Friendly villages, terraced field, green pasture of grassland and rich in fauna including medical herbs, rhododendron forests. Visitors can also visit honey-hunting place in Ghanpokhara village. So, we recommend our clients to visit once these places for a life time memory.
